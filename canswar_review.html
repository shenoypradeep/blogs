"CanSWar" - A Must-Grab Book — Light, Honest, and Deeply Uplifting

Book Review

This is a must grab book — for the following reasons:

  • It's very light, literally.

  • Can be completed in 1 or 2 sittings — very crisp.

  • The author just speaks to you in a simple, easy conversation — free of any jargon or attempt to sound articulate.

  • Each chapter begins like a movie scene, with epic quotes from Ancient Indian Spiritual Traditions like the Bhagavad Gita — befitting the chapter’s content, and filled with wisdom for a lifetime!

    • Although this book is about the ordeal with Cancer and the enlightenment that followed, it’s much more than that.





    Right from the outset, through to the end, Can-Swar has a gentle “Can-Do” Swar (or ring) to it. From the narration of childhood memories, to growing-up years, early career, marriage, and all the way through his ordeal with Cancer — the simple, frank conversation flow gives a glimpse into the mind and heart of Satyan ji.

    Throughout, we find a happy soul — content with what he gets, simple, grateful for the joys, open-minded about his sorrows, sincere to his family and friends, and brutally honest about his trials, ordeals, and shortcomings.

    It’s this sincerity — true to his name “Satyan” (the truthful one) — and his grateful heart, that seems to be the source of his strength, garnering the love and support of his friends and family, and seeing him through every ordeal into new life.

    Though he says his spiritual life, meeting Guru and going deeper into scriptures etc. happened after his bout with Cancer, it’s clear that he has always been even-minded through all of life’s ups and downs... willing to learn, to share his learnings, and to turn every experience into something meaningful for others.

    It feels as though the spiritual life he was already living simply deepened through the Cancer experience — finding its Swar, its Voice, and resonance in the wisdom of our divine scriptures, which he later explored more closely.

    Like Ekalavya of the yore, it’s the shraddhā, bhakti, and one-pointed dedication in the disciple that takes them to glorious spiritual heights.

    Our very own Spiritual Satyan Chetta — thus, has no qualms in calling me, someone 20 years younger than him, his “Spiritual Guru”!

    Starting Vedic Space with Satyan ji is truly a precious privilege — as all of us will get a rare opportunity to learn from him how to be the perfect student of life — simple, humble, and ever devoted to Divine Grace.

    Hari Om
